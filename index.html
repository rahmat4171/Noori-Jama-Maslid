About Us

A Beacon of Faith and Unity in the Heart of Kharka

Nestled in the serene and verdant landscape of Sitamarhi district, the Noori Jama Masjid of Kharka stands as more than just a house of worship; it is the spiritual nucleus and the pulsating heart of our community. Located in Ward No-20 of the village of Kharka, under the Punaura post office, our mosque is a sanctuary of peace, a center for learning, and a testament to the enduring faith and collective will of the people it serves. For generations, it has been a spiritual anchor, a place where the call to prayer (Adhan) marks the rhythm of our daily lives, and where the community gathers not only to bow before Allah (SWT) but also to strengthen the bonds of brotherhood and sisterhood that tie us together.

This Masjid was born from a shared dream—a collective aspiration of the elders and residents of Kharka to establish a central place for congregational prayer and Islamic learning. It was founded on the principles of piety, unity, and service. The journey from a humble vision to the structure that stands today is a story of immense sacrifice, unwavering dedication, and countless hours of voluntary effort from the local community. Every brick laid and every rupee contributed carries with it a prayer and a hope for divine reward. It represents the community's profound understanding that a mosque is the cornerstone of a vibrant Islamic society, a place where faith is nurtured, knowledge is imparted, and character is built.

Architectural Grace and Spiritual Ambiance

While modest in its design, the architecture of Noori Jama Masjid reflects a beautiful blend of traditional Islamic motifs and local craftsmanship. The elegant minaret, from which the muezzin’s call echoes across the village, reaches towards the heavens, serving as a constant reminder of our connection to the Divine. The central prayer hall, with its clean lines and uncluttered space, is designed to foster a sense of tranquility and focus. The Mihrab, the niche indicating the direction of the Kaaba in Makkah, is the focal point of the hall, a symbol of our united direction in worship. The Minbar, from which the Imam delivers the Friday sermon (Khutbah), stands as a platform for guidance, knowledge, and community address.

Upon entering the Masjid, one is immediately enveloped by an atmosphere of profound serenity. The air is thick with the recitation of the Holy Quran and the quiet hum of dhikr (remembrance of Allah). It is a space where the worries of the world are left at the doorstep, and the soul is free to commune with its Creator. The cool floors offer comfort to the prostrating worshipper, and the gentle light filtering through the windows illuminates the faces of the devout, creating a tapestry of devotion that is both humbling and inspiring.

The Pillars of Our Community Life

Salah (Prayer): The five daily congregational prayers—Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, and Isha—are the bedrock of our daily schedule. These prayers are moments of collective spiritual rejuvenation, where people from all walks of life stand shoulder-to-shoulder, equal before Allah, reaffirming their faith and seeking His guidance and mercy. The largest gathering is for the Jumu'ah (Friday) prayer, where the Imam’s sermon addresses pertinent spiritual, ethical, and social issues, providing guidance and fostering a sense of shared purpose and responsibility within the community.

Ilm (Knowledge): We firmly believe that the pursuit of knowledge is an obligation upon every Muslim. The Masjid serves as a vital educational center for our children. In the evenings, the halls come alive with the sounds of young voices learning to recite the Quran with correct Tajweed, studying the basics of the Arabic language, and learning the fundamental tenets of our faith in our Maktab program. This foundational education is crucial for nurturing a generation that is deeply connected to its Islamic identity and equipped to live a life guided by divine principles.

Community Service and Unity: Beyond its role as a place of prayer, the Masjid is the central hub for our community’s social life. It is a place where disputes are mediated and resolved with wisdom and compassion. It is where we come together to celebrate joyous occasions like Nikah (marriage) ceremonies, sharing in the happiness of our brothers and sisters. It is also where we gather to offer condolences and support to families during times of bereavement, sharing in their grief and offering prayers for the departed.

Ramadan and Festivals: The month of Ramadan brings a special spiritual fervor to the Masjid. The days are filled with fasting and recitation of the Quran, while the nights are illuminated by the special Taraweeh prayers. The community comes together for Iftar, breaking their fast in a spirit of generosity and togetherness. The blessed final nights of Ramadan, especially Laylat al-Qadr, are observed with immense devotion and lengthy prayers. The joyous occasions of Eid al-Fitr and Eid al-Adha are celebrated with special congregational prayers in the morning, where the entire community, dressed in their finest clothes, comes together to celebrate the culmination of their worship and to share greetings of peace and joy.

Our Vision for the Future and Your Role

As we look to the future, our vision for Noori Jama Masjid is one of continued growth and enhanced service to our community. We aspire to expand our educational programs, to provide more resources for our youth, and to increase our charitable activities to support the needy and vulnerable in our area. We envision a Masjid that is not only a beacon of spiritual light for Muslims but also a source of peace and goodwill for the entire wider community of Sitamarhi.

This noble vision cannot be realized without the help of Allah and the generous support of our community members and well-wishers like you. Your contributions, whether through prayer, voluntary service, or financial donations, are the lifeblood of this institution. Every donation helps us maintain the house of Allah, pay for essential utilities, support our Imam, and fund our educational and community programs. We invite you to be a part of this ongoing sadaqah jariyah (continuous charity) and to earn immense rewards from Allah (SWT).

We extend a warm and open invitation to all to visit us, to pray with us, and to experience the tranquility and brotherhood that defines Noori Jama Masjid Kharka. May Allah accept our efforts and make this Masjid a source of guidance and mercy for generations to come. Ameen.
